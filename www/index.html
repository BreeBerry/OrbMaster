⚔️ How to Play
OrbMaster is a code-breaking RPG. Each boss guards a secret sequence of colored orbs — crack the code before you run out of turns.
The Core Loop
1. Choose your boss from the Campaign screen. Bosses unlock in order as you defeat each one.
2. Set your loadout — equip up to 4 ability orbs from your stash before entering battle.
3. Study the Orb Pool — each boss has a fixed set of colors their code is drawn from, shown in the pre-battle loadout.
4. Submit a guess — place colored orbs into the slots and tap Submit.
5. Read the feedback pegs — they tell you how close your guess was.
6. Use orb abilities strategically to gain hints, extra turns, or eliminate colors.
7. Crack the code within the turn limit to earn orb fragments.
8. Spend fragments in the Orb Forge to craft powerful ability orbs.
Win & Loss
Win: Every orb in your guess is the correct color in the correct position.
Lose: Run out of turns without cracking the code.
Feedback Pegs
Red Peg — correct color, correct position
White Peg — correct color, wrong position
No Peg — color not in the code at all
Pegs show only the total count of reds and whites — not which slot each peg belongs to.
Turn Limit
Each match starts with 10 turns. Orb abilities can add extra turns. Some boss specials can remove them.
The faster you crack the code, the more fragments you earn.
🔒 Orb Locking
Double-tap a guess slot to lock that color to that position. Locked orbs carry forward into every future guess automatically. A gold glow marks locked slots. Double-tap again to unlock.
✕ Color Elimination
Triple-tap a color in the picker to eliminate it — graying it out when you're confident it's not in the sequence. Double-tap a grayed color to restore it.
Locked and eliminated colors are automatically filtered out by orb abilities — so hints and reveals focus on orbs you haven't figured out yet.
MindBreaker Altar
The MindBreaker Altar is your guess console — a stone altar with orb slots carved into its face. It determines how many orb slots you can use per guess.
Campaign Mode
In Campaign, each boss sets the code size automatically based on their difficulty. Bosses 1–7 use 4-orb codes, Bosses 8–9 use 5-orb codes, and Boss 10 uses a 6-orb code. The MindBreaker rank does not affect Campaign matches.
Multiplayer Mode
In Play Online (Multiplayer), the host chooses the code size — 4, 5, or 6 orbs. Both players must use the same code size. Your MindBreaker rank determines which sizes you can access.
4 Slots — Available from the start.
5 Slots — Requires Rank 5 upgrade from the Orb Forge.
6 Slots — Requires Rank 6 upgrade from the Orb Forge.
If your opponent's chosen code size exceeds your rank, you'll see a warning. Ask them to lower the size or upgrade your altar first.
Upgrade Costs
Rank 5 (5 slots): 150 Orange + 150 Blue + 100 Green + 100 Red fragments. Requires beating Boss 5.
Rank 6 (6 slots): 200 Purple + 150 White + 150 Black + 100 Teal fragments. Requires beating Boss 7.
Deeper codes earn bigger fragment rewards — ×1.3 for 5 slots, ×1.7 for 6 slots.

👹 Boss Profiles
Each boss guards a secret orb code. Defeat them in order to progress the campaign and unlock new colors, ranks, and abilities.
Fragment System
Orb Fragments are the currency of OrbMaster. You earn them by winning matches and spend them in the Orb Forge to craft powerful ability orbs.
Earning Fragments
Each match rewards 4 batches of fragments when you win. The type and amount depend on how quickly you solved the code and the boss's loot table.
Base reward per orb: 50 fragments
Turn penalty: −5 fragments per orb for every turn used after Turn 1
Formula: (50 − ((turns_used − 2) × 5)) × code_size × multiplier
Solving in Turn 1 or 2 gives the maximum reward. Using orb abilities that add turns does not reduce your reward.
Reward Multipliers
5-orb code: ×1.3 bonus to all fragments earned
6-orb code: ×1.7 bonus to all fragments earned
Boss 7+: +20% bonus fragments (late-game economy boost)
Loot Rarity
Each fragment batch is rolled against the boss's loot table to determine its rarity tier — Common, Uncommon, or Rare. Higher rarity means rarer orb types appear in the reward.
Silver Orb (+15% Uncommon) and Gold Orb (+20% Rare) passively shift the loot table in your favor when equipped.
Forging Orbs
Visit the Orb Forge to spend fragments and craft ability orbs. Each orb has its own fragment cost — any orb can be forged once you have enough fragments, regardless of campaign progress.
🗺️ Progression & Unlocks
OrbMaster has a full Campaign of 10 bosses. Defeat each boss to unlock the next. No fragment requirements — just beat the previous boss to advance.
Boss Orb Pool Progression
Each boss draws their secret code from a fixed orb pool. The pool grows with each boss, adding one new color per boss from Boss 4 onward.
Bosses 1–3: Red, Blue, Yellow, Green, Orange (5 orbs)
Boss 4: + Silver (6 orbs)
Boss 5: + Gold (7 orbs)
Boss 6: + Purple (8 orbs)
Boss 7: + Black (9 orbs)
Boss 8: + White (10 orbs) — 5-orb code
Boss 9: + Pink (11 orbs) — 5-orb code
Boss 10: + Teal (12 orbs) — 6-orb code
The full orb pool for each boss is shown in the pre-battle loadout screen. You'll only see those colors in the battle picker.
MindBreaker Altar (Multiplayer)
The altar rank only affects Multiplayer code size selection. Campaign code sizes are fixed by each boss.
Rank 5 (5 slots): Purchase at Orb Forge after Boss 5.
Rank 6 (6 slots): Purchase at Orb Forge after Boss 7.
Settings
Duplicate Colors: Toggle in Settings to allow or disallow repeated colors in boss codes. On by default — turning it off makes codes harder to guess.
Build Archetypes
🧠 Solver Build: Black + Red + Green — maximum information per turn.
💰 Economy Build: Gold + Silver — boost your loot rarity for bigger fragment rewards.
🛡️ Control Build: Yellow + Blue + Purple — board manipulation and extra turns.
💪 Endurance Build: Orange + Purple + White — stack extra turns for harder bosses.
🌐 Play Online
OrbMaster's multiplayer lets you battle other players or the MindBreaker AI in code-cracking duels. Both players set a secret code for each other — first to crack the opponent's code wins.
Challenge a Friend (Room Codes)
Create Room: Tap "Create Room" to generate a 6-character room code. Share the code with your opponent.
Join Room: Tap "Join Room" and enter the code to connect. Once both players are in, the host starts the match.
Code Size: The host selects the code size (4, 5, or 6 orbs) before creating the room. Both players use the same size. Your MindBreaker rank must support the chosen size.
Orb Pool Difficulty: The host also selects the orb pool — Easy (5 colors), Medium (8), Hard (10), Full (12), or Custom. Both players draw from the same pool.
Find Random Match
Tap "Find Random Match" to queue up and be automatically paired with another player searching at the same time.
🏆 Special Abilities (Post-Campaign)
Defeat all 10 campaign bosses to unlock Multiplayer Specials in the Orb Forge — single-use, boss-style attacks for Play Online. Own as many as you like, but equip only one. Tap your Special's golden button during battle to unleash it on your opponent.
❄️ Temple Ice — Freezes a random orb color in your opponent's picker for 2 rounds. 250 Blue + 250 White
🌫️ Brute Fog — Hides all peg feedback on your opponent's next guess. 250 Green + 250 Black
🔀 Nano Swap — Swaps two orbs in your opponent's next submitted guess. 300 Silver + 200 Orange
👑 Royal Transmute — Transmutes one orb in your opponent's next guess into a random color. 250 Purple + 250 Gold
🌀 Feral Scramble — Scramble the order of your OWN code, resetting your opponent's progress. Usable only in the first 4 rounds. 250 Teal + 250 Pink
Specials work in Vs Computer matches too — but beware, the MindBreaker AI mirrors your equipped Special and will fire it back at you. Equip or swap your Special from the Orb Forge.
A pulsing search animation shows while you wait. The system will match you within 60 seconds or time out.
You can cancel the search at any time by tapping "Cancel."
Random matches use the default code size (4) and a standard orb pool. For custom settings, use room codes instead.
Play vs MindBreaker AI
Tap "Play vs MindBreaker AI" for a casual practice match against the AI. Choose your code size, orb pool, and whether to enable orb abilities.
AI matches are not ranked and don't affect your campaign progress. They're ideal for testing loadouts and strategies.
Friends List & Invites
Your Google Play Games friends appear at the bottom of the multiplayer lobby. If a friend is online and has a room open, you'll see a "Join" button next to their name.
Inviting: When you create a room, you can invite friends directly. They'll see a banner notification with your room code and can join with one tap.
Online Presence: A green dot indicates friends who are currently in the multiplayer lobby. Tap "Refresh" to update the list.
Friends are loaded from your Google Play Games account. You must be signed in to see and invite friends.
Add friends from matches: After a random online match, tap Add Friend on the results screen. If your opponent accepts, they join your friends list — then you can challenge them directly from the Friends section, no room code needed.
Multiplayer Battle Flow
1. Set Your Code: Both players pick a secret orb sequence for their opponent to crack.
2. Crack Their Code: Take turns guessing your opponent's code. Feedback pegs work the same as Campaign mode.
3. Winner: The first player to crack the code wins. If both crack it on the same turn, the player who used fewer total turns wins.
Your equipped orb abilities are active during multiplayer battles. Choose your loadout wisely!
Navigation
The back button in multiplayer is phase-aware: it returns you to the previous step rather than jumping straight to the home screen. From the lobby, back exits multiplayer entirely.
🎮 Google Play Games
OrbMaster integrates with Google Play Games Services for your player profile, friends list, and cloud saves. Sign-in happens automatically when the app starts.
Sign-In
Automatic: OrbMaster attempts to sign in to your Google Play Games account when the app launches. If you're signed into Play Games on your device, you're signed in — a confirmation appears and your name shows under your avatar on the title screen.
Manual: If automatic sign-in fails or you skipped it, tap the Sign in pill under your avatar on the title screen, or use the Google Play Games → Sign In button in Settings.
If sign-in fails (no Play Games account, offline, etc.), the game continues in local mode with emoji avatars and local saves.
Player Profile
When signed in, your Google Play Games display name and avatar replace the default emoji profile. Your PGS avatar appears on the title screen and in multiplayer.
Open your profile to see your PGS name, avatar, and cloud sync status. Profile details come from your Google Play Games account and are read-only in OrbMaster.
If not signed in, you can still set a local emoji avatar and display name from the profile screen.
Cloud Saves
When signed in, your game progress is automatically saved to the cloud via Play Games Saved Games. Every time the game saves locally, a cloud backup is queued.
Cloud saves include all campaign progress, fragments, forged orbs, loadouts, and settings.
Sync on sign-in: When you sign in on a new device, OrbMaster checks for a cloud save. If found, it loads your progress so you can pick up where you left off.
Conflict resolution: If both a local save and cloud save exist, the most recently modified save wins.
Cloud saves require an active Google Play Games sign-in. If you play offline, your progress saves locally and syncs to the cloud next time you're connected and signed in.
Per-Player Saves
Game progress is keyed to your Play Games player ID. If multiple players share the same device, each signed-in player has their own independent save file.
Switching Google accounts on the device will load that account's save data automatically.
